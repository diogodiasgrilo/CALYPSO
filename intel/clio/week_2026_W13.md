## Part 3: Strategy Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Emergency State-Machine Fix — `fomc_announcement_skip` Auto-Clear
**Priority: CRITICAL**

**Data citation:** HYDRA idled on 2026-03-24, 03-25, 03-26, and 03-27 due to a stale `fomc_announcement_skip` flag. The legitimate FOMC blackout was March 18 (Day 2). The flag persisted for **at least 7 calendar days** past its valid window. Apollo explicitly flagged the bug on 03-25, 03-26, and 03-27; the fix was not applied before any of those sessions.

**Recommendation:** Implement an automatic flag expiry: `fomc_announcement_skip` must auto-clear to `false` at midnight following any FOMC Day 2 date. Alternatively, compute the flag dynamically from the FOMC calendar at session start rather than persisting it in state. The state file should not be the authoritative source for FOMC status — the hardcoded calendar should be.

**Confidence: HIGH** — Four idle days of confirmed data, same root cause each day, Apollo identified it correctly all three days it warned.

**Expected impact:** 4 idle sessions this week alone. At average recent credit of ~$1,500/day (estimated from nearby comparable sessions — *note: this is an approximation from available sheets data, not a precise figure*), this is a significant opportunity cost. Cannot be precisely quantified from this week's data alone since the foregone sessions' actual P&L is unknown.

### Recommendation 2: Apollo Accuracy Flag — Add Operational Mode for Idle Days
**Priority: MEDIUM**

**Data citation:** Cheat_sheet flagged Apollo `accurate: false` on 03-26 and 03-27 with the note "RED but positive day." Apollo's actual prediction on both days was that HYDRA would idle unless the flag was cleared — which was correct. The current flag logic evaluates market outcome (did a RED day have a negative result?) rather than operational outcome (did Apollo correctly characterize system and market state?).

**Recommendation:** Add an `operational_prediction_accurate` field to the cheat sheet separate from `market_prediction_accurate`. On days with 0 entries, set `market_prediction_accurate: null` (not testable) and evaluate `operational_prediction_accurate` on whether Apollo correctly diagnosed system status. This prevents the accuracy metric from being polluted by untradeable days.

**Confidence: HIGH** — Three idle-day misclassifications this week directly support this.

### Recommendation 3: MKT-028 Spread Width Bug — Escalate to Critical Defect
**Priority: HIGH**

**Data citation:** HERMES flagged `spread_width: 50` on all 11 position legs on 2026-03-23, noting "Day 4 of a documented out-of-spec condition." The required parameters are 60pt call floor and 75pt put floor. All 5 put stops on 03-23 triggered on 50pt put spreads instead of 75pt spreads.

**Recommendation:** The spread width parameter (`spread_width`) must be corrected to enforce MKT-028 minimums: 60pt calls, 75pt puts. This is not a strategy parameter change — it is a bug fix. The 75pt put spread was specifically chosen (per strategy spec) to provide more dollar buffer before stop activation. Running 50pt put spreads undercuts that design assumption. Until fixed, all stop analysis on put legs is against a non-spec structure.

**Confidence: HIGH** — Data shows Day 4 of a confirmed deviation as of 03-23. Impact on stop outcomes cannot be precisely isolated from available data but is directionally negative (less buffer).

## Part 4: New Learnings for Strategy Memory

**Derived exclusively from W13 data (2026-03-23 through 2026-03-27). Each references specific data.**

1. **Ghost FOMC flag caused 4 consecutive idle days (03-24 through 03-27).** The `fomc_announcement_skip` flag set during the March 18 FOMC blackout was never cleared, causing HYDRA to idle on four normal trading days. The last valid blackout was March 18; the flag persisted until at least March 27 (Day +7). Apollo correctly identified the bug on 03-25, 03-26, and 03-27 but the fix was not applied before any session. Root cause: `fomc_announcement_skip` is persisted in the state file rather than computed dynamically from the FOMC calendar. This is the highest-priority known defect as of W13.

2. **VIX futures/cash spread of 2.4pt observed on 03-23 (Apollo: 26.85, cash open: 24.45).** Apollo's pre-market VIX reading from overnight futures overestimated the actual cash VIX at the open by 2.4pt. This creates a scenario where E1 strike placement (using live cash VIX of ~24.45) is modestly tighter than Apollo's pre-market projection suggested. When futures VIX materially exceeds expected cash VIX, Apollo's implied strike ranges are overstated for the early entries.

3. **On 03-23, all 5 full ICs stopped on the put side despite VIX-elevated credit clearing MKT-011 floors with margin (put credits ranged $2.10–$2.65).** SPX fell 87pt from high to low. Elevated credit at entry did not correlate with safety — it reflected the market's correct pricing of the directional risk that materialised. In sustained selloff sessions (VIX >24, gap-up fade, 87pt high-to-low range), put-side MKT-011 credit viability is not a useful predictor of stop avoidance.

4. **MKT-028 spread width anomaly (50pt actual vs. 60/75pt spec) was in its 4th consecutive day of being unresolved as of 03-23.** All 11 position legs on 03-23 show `spread_width: 50`. The five put stops that occurred on that day triggered at lower dollar thresholds than the 75pt spec would have produced. Any stop analysis from approximately 03-17 onward may be against a non-spec structure; backtest assumptions using 75pt put spreads do not apply to this period.

5. **Apollo's `accurate: false` classification fires on idle days when "RED but positive day" is the cheat_sheet note.** This occurred on 03-26 and 03-27, both idle sessions where Apollo's primary prediction was operational (system will idle due to ghost flag). The current accuracy flag logic evaluates market outcome against risk level, which is not meaningful when zero positions are placed. Apollo's operational predictions on both days (system will idle) were correct. The accuracy metric is being polluted by untradeable sessions.
